Customer Churn Analysis

Machine Learning

VerdaBank faced a 20.37% churn rate, exceeding the 19% industry benchmark. Analysis of 10,000 customers revealed that churn is primarily driven by shallow product relationships and customer inactivity, with single-product inactive customers showing 1.68x higher odds of churning and churning at nearly 2x the rate of active customers.

Python · Logistic Regression · Feature Engineering
View Deck →

📊 Project Details

Background

VerdaBank faced a 20.37% churn rate exceeding the 19% industry benchmark across 10,000 customers in France, Germany, and Spain, with a target of 15% within 12 months. The analysis aimed to identify behavioral and demographic drivers of churn through systematic investigation. Process: Data cleaning and standardization of 10,000 records, feature engineering to capture behavioral patterns, exploratory data analysis across activity status, product depth, geography, and demographics, logistic regression validation to quantify churn risk, and scenario simulations to estimate intervention impact.

Method / Strategy / Solution / Metrics

Tools: Python (Google Colab), Tableau, Kaggle Dataset.
Methods: Data Preparation, Feature Engineering, EDA, Logistic Regression, Scenario Simulation.
Strategy: Prioritize 2nd Product Adoption through "product deepening" programs for single-product holders (highest churn risk). Launch proactive engagement campaigns for "Inactive" customers. Shift from demographic targeting to behavioral targeting using Activity Index as primary metric for retention efforts.

Result / Output

Primary Churn Driver: Customer disengagement and shallow product relationships are the strongest predictors of churn.
The "Single Product" Risk: Inactive customers holding only one product face the highest risk of leaving, with an Odds Ratio of 1.68.
Demographic Neutrality: Financial, demographic, and geographic attributes do not independently explain churn as strongly as behavior does.
Impact Potential: Converting 30% of single-product customers into multi-product users is estimated to reduce the overall churn rate by 1.62%.
Scope: End-to-end analysis including business definition, data preparation & validation, feature engineering, logistic regression modeling, odds ratio quantification, intervention scenario simulation, strategic roadmap development, and actionable retention prioritization.

📊 Project Details

Background

RevoBank analyzed six months of credit card transaction data (8,950 records) to optimize profitability from a 1.5% transaction margin. The project focused on creating actionable user personas through data cleaning, handling missing values, and applying K-Means Clustering to group customers based on spending behavior. Process: Normalized customer metrics, applied unsupervised clustering to discover natural groupings, and profiled segments across revenue, activity, and fraud dimensions.

Method / Strategy / Solution / Metrics

Tools: Python (Pandas, NumPy, Scikit-Learn), Matplotlib/Seaborn.
Methods: Data Cleaning, Feature Engineering, K-Means Clustering (k=3), Customer Profiling.
Strategy: Segment 1 Reactivation—deploy specific programs for "Dormant" customers who are financially capable but low in activity. Premium Penetration—prioritize Segment 2 (High-Value) for premium offerings to maximize the 1.5% margin. Service Quality Retention—maintain high standards for Segment 0 (Conservative) without aggressive upselling.

Result / Output

Persona Identification: Successfully segmented 8,950 customers into 3 distinct personas: Conservative, Dormant, and High-Value.
High-Value Opportunity: Identified 162 High-Value customers with highest credit limits and active behavior, representing primary profit-driving segment.
Dormant Segment Potential: Segment 1 (Dormant) consists of financially capable customers with solid credit scores but low activity—major reactivation opportunity.
Profit Margin Anchoring: All strategies anchored to 1.5% profit margin per transaction.
Scope: Data preparation & standardization, cluster analysis with k=3 optimization, segment profiling (revenue, margin, fraud), business interpretation, and strategic recommendations for activation and fraud mitigation.

Corporate Profit Crisis

Business Analytics

Revoshion experienced a 13.6% profit decline despite 20.4% sales growth, indicating structural margin erosion. Root cause analysis revealed that excessive discounting emerged as the primary margin drain, growing faster than sales volume, while the commission structure misalignment incentivizes volume over profitability.

Margin Analysis · KPI Breakdown · Root Cause Framework
View Deck →

📊 Project Details

Background

Revoshion experienced a profit crisis with a 13.6% decline despite 20.4% sales growth, indicating structural margin erosion. Using the Superstore Dashboard, the analysis applied an Issue Tree for root cause identification to uncover factors driving margin decline. Process: Decomposed profit variance into components (discount, commission, product mix) to isolate root causes, analyzed margin trends over time to identify inflection points, benchmarked current state against historical performance to quantify impact magnitude, and developed policy-level interventions targeting structural issues.

Method / Strategy / Solution / Metrics

Tools: Superstore Dashboard (Priya Padham), Excel/PowerPoint.
Methods: Root Cause Analysis (Issue Tree), DARCI Model, Trend Analysis, Policy Review.
Solution & Strategy: Implement discount caps at 15% to control margin drain. Revise commission structure from volume-based to profitability-based incentives. Optimize product mix toward high-margin categories. Target: Recover 13.6% loss and achieve 10% profit growth within 12 months.

Result / Output

Profit-Growth Gap: Identified critical performance gap where corporate profits declined by 13.6% despite 20.4% sales increase.
Root Cause - Discounting: High average discount percentages were primary driver of margin erosion, growing faster than sales volume.
Suboptimal Product Mix: High sales volume concentrated in low-margin basic products, preventing conversion into profit growth.
Policy Interventions: Discount caps and commission realignment projected to recover 13.6% loss and achieve 10% target within 12 months.
Scope: Margin analysis across product lines, discount trending, commission evaluation, comparative benchmarking, and policy-level recommendations targeting margin recovery.

📊 Project Details

Background

TokoBli evaluated the efficiency of three "Twin-Date" promotional campaigns (10/10, 11/11, 12/12) across 10,159 transactions to optimize future marketing spend. The project aimed to increase revenue by 10% through A/B Testing (T-test) on transaction data. Process: Cleaned transaction data to remove outliers, developed efficiency metric (RoD) to compare campaigns, benchmarked campaigns across efficiency/revenue/cost dimensions, and modeled hybrid approach combining each strategy's strengths.

Method / Strategy / Solution / Metrics

Tools: Excel/Google Sheets (F-test, T-test), PowerPoint.
Methods: Descriptive Statistics, A/B Testing, Hypothesis Testing (One-tailed T-test), Data Cleaning, Return on Discount (RoD) Analysis.
Strategy: Deploy new product page design (proven P-value 0.0%). Implement Campaign 12/12 efficiency (58.54% RoD) as benchmark. Model hybrid approach combining Campaign 11/11 revenue scale with Campaign 12/12 spending discipline.

Result / Output

Design Significance: New Product Page Design proven extremely effective with P-value 0.0%, far below 5% threshold.
T-Stat Magnitude: T-stat of 8.008 indicates transaction value difference ~8x greater than standard error.
Campaign Efficiency: Campaign 12/12 achieved 58.54% RoD with 1.71% cost ratio (most cost-efficient). Campaign 11/11 delivered 82.3% RoD with 3.24% cost ratio (highest revenue).
Revenue Goal: Validated data-driven path to achieve 10% revenue increase.
Scope: Transaction cleaning & validation, campaign benchmarking via RoD, cost efficiency assessment, hybrid strategy modeling, revenue projection under controlled discount spending.

📊 Project Details

Background

This project developed an interactive Tableau dashboard to help RevoFinance users monitor their personal spending habits. By comparing actual expenses against planned budgets, the visualization identifies areas of overspending through monthly trend analysis and transaction distribution, supporting smarter financial decision-making for individual users.

Method / Strategy / Solution / Metrics

Tools: Tableau, Excel (Data Source).
Methods: Data Visualization, Dashboard Design, Trend Analysis, Budget vs. Actual Analysis, Seasonal Pattern Detection.
Strategy: Year-End Planned Allocation ("December Buffer") to manage seasonal spike. Frequency monitoring of mid-range transactions (Rp 250K-750K). Discretionary optimization focusing on "Wants" category control.

Result / Output

Spending Frequency: Most transactions fall in Rp 250K–750K range, driven by frequent mid-value purchases not large outliers.
Seasonal Volatility: Spending stable year-round but spikes sharply in December, creating significant budget pressure.
Budget Stability: Essential categories (Utilities, Transport, Groceries) predictable at 77–83% of budget.
Overspending Detection: Interactive dashboard identifies overspending risks early via actual vs. planned budget comparison.
Process & Scope: Data integration (transactions + budget), dashboard design, LOD metric development, merchant/category analysis, trend/seasonal pattern detection, and budget alerts.
