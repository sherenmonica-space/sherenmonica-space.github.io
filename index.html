Customer Churn Analysis

Machine Learning

VerdaBank faced a 20.37% churn rate, exceeding the 19% industry benchmark. Analysis of 10,000 customers revealed that churn is primarily driven by shallow product relationships and customer inactivity, with single-product inactive customers showing 1.68x higher odds of churning and churning at nearly 2x the rate of active customers.

Python · Logistic Regression · Feature Engineering

📊 Project Details

Project Overview

This project analyzed VerdaBank's 20.37% churn rate, which exceeded the 19% industry benchmark, using a dataset of 10,000 customers across France, Germany, and Spain. Aiming for a 15% churn target within 12 months, the process involved data cleaning in Tableau, exploratory data analysis (EDA), and validation via Logistic Regression to identify how behavioral and demographic factors impact customer loyalty.

Tools & Method

Tools: Python (Google Colab), Tableau, Kaggle Dataset.
Methods: Data Preparation, Feature Engineering, EDA, Logistic Regression, Scenario Simulation.

Approach

Cleaned and standardized 10,000 customer records. Applied feature engineering to capture behavioral patterns and interaction effects. Conducted EDA across activity status, product depth, geography, and demographics. Validated drivers via logistic regression to quantify churn risk. Performed scenario simulations to estimate intervention impact.

Results & Insights

Primary Churn Driver: Customer disengagement and shallow product relationships are the strongest predictors of churn.
The "Single Product" Risk: Inactive customers holding only one product face the highest risk of leaving, with an Odds Ratio of 1.68.
Demographic Neutrality: Financial, demographic, and geographic attributes do not independently explain churn as strongly as behavior does.
Impact Potential: Converting 30% of single-product customers into multi-product users is estimated to reduce the overall churn rate by 1.62%.

Recommendations

Prioritize 2nd Product Adoption: Focus on "product deepening" programs for single-product holders, as they represent the highest churn risk.
Proactive Engagement: Launch automated reactivation campaigns for "Inactive" customers before they reach the high-risk churn window.
Behavioral Targeting: Shift focus from demographic profiles to engagement status (Activity Index) as the primary metric for retention efforts.

Scope of Work

End-to-end analysis: business definition, data preparation & validation across 10,000 records, feature engineering & EDA, logistic regression modeling, odds ratio quantification, intervention scenario simulation, strategic roadmap development, and actionable retention prioritization (reactivation vs. product conversion strategies).

📊 Project Details

Project Overview

This analysis evaluated six months of RevoBank's credit card transaction data to boost profitability from a 1.5% transaction margin. Using a dataset of 8,950 records, the project focused on creating actionable user personas through data cleaning, handling missing values, and applying K-Means Clustering to group customers based on their spending behavior.

Tools & Method

Tools: Python (Pandas, NumPy, Scikit-Learn), Matplotlib/Seaborn.
Methods: Data Cleaning, Feature Engineering, K-Means Clustering, Customer Profiling.

Approach

Normalized customer metrics and applied unsupervised clustering to discover natural customer groupings. Profiled each segment across revenue, activity, and fraud dimensions. Identified growth and risk mitigation opportunities within each segment.

Results & Insights

Persona Identification: Successfully segmented 8,950 customers into 3 distinct personas: Conservative, Dormant, and High-Value.
High-Value Opportunity: Identified 162 High-Value customers who possess the highest credit limits and active transaction behavior, representing the primary profit-driving segment.
Dormant Segment Potential: Segment 1 (Dormant) consists of financially capable customers with solid credit scores but low activity, representing a major reactivation opportunity.
Profit Margin: All strategies are anchored to the bank's 1.5% profit margin earned per credit card transaction.

Recommendations

Segment 1 Reactivation: Deploy specific performance programs to re-engage "Dormant" customers who are financially capable but low in activity.
Premium Penetration: Prioritize Segment 2 (High-Value) for premium product offerings to maximize the 1.5% profit margin per transaction.
Service Quality Retention: Maintain high service standards for Segment 0 (Conservative) without aggressive upselling to ensure long-term stability.

Scope of Work

Data preparation & standardization, cluster analysis with k=3 optimization, segment profiling (revenue, margin, fraud), business interpretation, and strategic recommendations for activation and fraud mitigation.

Revoshion experienced a 13.6% profit decline despite 20.4% sales growth, indicating structural margin erosion. 📊 Project Details

Project Overview

This project diagnosed Revoshion's corporate profit crisis, where profits fell 13.6% despite a 20.4% growth in sales. Using the Superstore Dashboard, the analysis utilized an Issue Tree for root cause identification to uncover the top factors driving margin decline and developed data-driven recommendations to restore profitability by 10% within a year.

Tools & Method

Tools: Superstore Dashboard (Priya Padham), Excel/PowerPoint.
Methods: Root Cause Analysis (Issue Tree), DARCI Model, Trend Analysis, Policy Review.

Approach

Decomposed profit variance into components (discount, commission, product mix) to isolate root causes. Analyzed margin trends over time to identify inflection points and policy changes driving deterioration. Benchmarked current state against historical performance to quantify impact magnitude. Developed policy-level interventions targeting structural issues rather than tactical adjustments.

Results & Insights

Profit-Growth Gap: Identified a critical performance gap where corporate profits declined by 13.6% despite a healthy 20.4% increase in sales.
Root Cause - Discounting: High average discount percentages per corporate order were found to be the primary driver of margin erosion.
Suboptimal Product Mix: High sales volume is currently concentrated in low-margin basic products, preventing volume from converting into profit growth.
Recovery Target: Internal policy interventions are projected to recover the 13.6% loss and achieve a 10% profit growth target within 12 months.

Scope of Work

Margin analysis across product lines, discount rate trending, commission structure evaluation, comparative benchmarking, and policy-level intervention recommendations targeting margin recovery.

📊 Project Details

Project Overview

This study evaluated the efficiency of TokoBli's "Twin-Date" campaigns (10/10, 11/11, 12/12) to optimize future marketing spend. By performing A/B Testing (T-test) on transaction data, the project aimed to increase revenue by 10% by comparing revenue-per-unit-discount and identifying the most cost-efficient campaign strategy.

Tools & Method

Tools: Excel/Google Sheets (F-test, T-test), PowerPoint.
Methods: Descriptive Statistics, A/B Testing, Hypothesis Testing (One-tailed T-test), Data Cleaning, Return on Discount (RoD) Analysis.

Approach

Cleaned transaction data to remove outliers before analysis. Developed efficiency metric (RoD) to compare campaigns on consistent basis. Benchmarked each campaign across multiple dimensions (efficiency, revenue, cost). Modeled hybrid approach combining strengths of each strategy to optimize both revenue and profitability simultaneously.

Results & Insights

Design Significance: The New Product Page Design was proven to be extremely effective with a P-value of 0.0%, far below the 5% significance threshold.
T-Stat Magnitude: A T-stat of 8.008 indicates that the difference in transaction value between the new and old design is approximately 8 times greater than the standard error.
Campaign Efficiency: Analysis of historical "Twin-Date" campaigns (10/10, 11/11, 12/12) identified specific scenarios that maximize revenue-per-unit-discount.
Revenue Goal: Validated a data-driven path to achieve a 10% revenue increase over the historical average.

Scope of Work

Transaction cleaning & validation, campaign performance benchmarking via RoD analysis, cost efficiency assessment, hybrid strategy modeling, and revenue projection under controlled discount spending.

Analysis revealed that spending is driven primarily by frequent mid-value transactions rather than large outliers, with most transactions falling in the Rp 250K–750K range, and a significant seasonal spike in December creating annual budget pressure.

📊 Project Details

Project Overview

This project developed an interactive Tableau dashboard to help RevoFinance users monitor their personal spending habits. By comparing actual expenses against planned budgets, the visualization identifies areas of overspending through monthly trend analysis and transaction distribution, supporting smarter financial decision-making for individual users.

Tools & Method

Tools: Tableau, Excel (Data Source).
Methods: Data Visualization, Dashboard Design, Trend Analysis, Budget vs. Actual Analysis, Seasonal Pattern Detection.

Approach

Built data model enabling dynamic filtering across multiple dimensions without losing aggregation consistency. Designed dashboard views to reveal spending patterns at transaction, merchant, and category levels. Incorporated time-based analysis to identify seasonal variations and early warning signals. Emphasized interactivity to enable self-service exploration of budget vs actual spending dynamics.

Results & Insights

Spending Frequency: Most transactions fall within the Rp 250K–750K range, showing that spending growth is driven by frequent mid-value purchases rather than rare large outliers.
Seasonal Volatility: Spending remains stable throughout the year but spikes sharply in December, creating significant budget pressure.
Budget Stability: Essential spending categories (Utilities, Transport, Groceries) are highly predictable, consistently staying within 77–83% of the budget.
Overspending Awareness: The interactive dashboard successfully identifies overspending risks early by comparing actual expenses against planned monthly budgets.

Scope of Work

Data integration (transactions and budget), dashboard design and layout, LOD metric development, merchant and category analysis, trend detection, seasonal pattern identification, and actionable budget alerts.
